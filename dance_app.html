Dance App

Overview

Background

Dancers complained of no free hands to capture moments while dancing, which is a typical problem. Other dancers said it was time consuming to review all of the videos and find quality clips, and most aspiring dancers said they were unable to get professional reviews or comments on videos.

Objective

We wanted it to be easier for the dancers to review and organize videos and pictures on the app to save time as well as make friends.

My Role

UI/UX Designer, Researcher

Scope

Semester project. 2019 Spring.

Research

Interviews

We interviewd 8 dancers, most were recreational, and the more serious dancers, the aspiring dancers and dance team captains all wanted a better way to record themselves dancing and a way to better themselves.

Competitive Analysis

We found 8 competitive apps which allowed video sharing, and all of them have strengths and weaknesses. All 8 apps allowed sharing photos and videos, but the apps did not allow users to rate the videos or request a portfolio review. We made an app that allows sharing videos to other users, look up dance classes, schedule classes, and chat with the students who are taking the same dance teacher.

Personas

We interviewed dancers and asked why they like to dance, what their goals are, and if they like to record those moments. These three personas are of people who dance regularly, use social media apps to record their dance steps, and take pictures. This information helps us understand their needs and what they want the most from social media apps they use.
